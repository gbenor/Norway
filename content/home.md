# Lital & Gilad Explore Norway

## A Journey Through Fjords, Mountains and Norwegian Culture

June 2026

The idea for this trip did not begin as a classic holiday plan. It started because Lital had an EMDR conference in Oslo. Instead of treating the journey as a short professional trip, we decided to take advantage of being in Norway and continue into a full road trip across the country.

At first, the plan sounded simple: arrive in Oslo, see the famous fjords, experience something close to the classic "Norway in a Nutshell" route, and finish in Bergen. But very quickly the trip became much more than a list of attractions.

For more than a week, it felt as if we were living inside a postcard. Every day brought a new combination of water, cliffs, snow, tunnels, ferries, villages, waterfalls and mountains. Sometimes the scenery looked too perfect to be real. A short drive could move us from a quiet city street to a forest, from a forest to a mountain road, from a mountain road to a fjord, and from a fjord into a tunnel that seemed to continue forever.

What stayed with us most was not only the beauty. It was the way Norway works with its geography. Where there are mountains, Norway builds tunnels. Where there are fjords, Norway uses ferries. Where there are waterfalls, Norway generates power. Where there is snow, Norway builds a culture around skiing. Nature in Norway is not just something people admire. It shapes the roads, the towns, the sports, the energy system and the identity of the country.

This manuscript is both a memory and a guide. It tells the story of what we saw and did, but also the questions we asked along the way: Why are the fjords salty? Why is Norway so rich? Why are there so many tunnels? How do you build a trail to Trolltunga? Why does a ferry feel like a road? Why are all the people in Vigeland Park naked?
